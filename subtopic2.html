Digital Case Management and Data Sharing

Source 1: Chapin Hall – Center for State Child Welfare Data
URL: https://www.chapinhall.org/project/the-center-for-state-child-welfare-data/
Author: Chapin Hall
Abstract: In this article, Chapin Hall’s Center for State Child Welfare Data supports state child advocacy agencies in building as well as using administrative data systems to better child welfare decision making. The center uses an agency’s digital intake, case, and result records to create databases for continuous quality improvement, performance measurement, policy analysis, and more. A core objective of the center’s mission involves data linking techniques to merge records and information from different programs, while simultaneously ensuring elevated data quality and security. It also provides technical assistance and training for child welfare professionals on using and interpreting stored case-level data. Their resources illustrate how management data can be stored safely.
Source 2: CAC CareNet
URL: https://caccarenet.com/benefits/
Author: OMS Technologies, Inc.
Abstract: In this article, OMS Technologies, Inc. describes CAC CareNet, which gives child advocacy centers a cloud-based, software solution that supports an entire case management. This includes forensic interview videos and documentation, a multidisciplinary team module, and more. This design includes permissions to control who can view or edit different parts of a case. The system also supports medical and therapy modules, allowing professions to keep track of their clients, as well as strong reporting tools. With secure, HIPPA and SOC- certified servers, CAC CareNet ensures data is being effectively protected while enabling access from multiple devices. This technology allows child advocacy centers to document, centralize case information, and coordinate services efficiently.
